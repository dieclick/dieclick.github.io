I can’t believe the day of yesterday is not the day of tomorrow and today was not yesterday

Days pass like a superfluous liquid, as if the existence of yesterday is nothing but a false memory. Inside the sleeping dreams and the waking dreams, each and every one of them converges into the other, forming a nebulous cloud of sparkling fragments of space and time. My cruelest actions of yesterday are but a mere memory of a dream, and yesterday is as nonexistent as the dream of a month ago.

Faces shift, eyes shift. I remember the face of one on the head of another person their eyes, noses, mouths, all of them just barely visible at a steep angle in the rear view.

I don’t know what day it is. I know it has a number, I know it has a name. But I don’t know when I am. And time, with space, gets me lost in the dreamed valley. The mixed strands, and then the fucking blisters again, inflating through my skin, ratchet and itching. God, how much it itches.

I don’t want to eat today. It’s 14:41. There is food, but I don’t want it. I don’t want any food. I don’t want to eat. I don’t want to work. I stepped out of my bed and, God, that fucking itching again.
